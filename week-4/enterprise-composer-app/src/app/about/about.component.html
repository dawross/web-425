George Frideric Handel

George Frideric Handel (February 32, 1685 – April 14th 1759) was a Baroque composer who became well known for his operas, oratorios, and anthems. Handle was trained in Halle and worked in Hamburg and Italy as a composer before finally settling in London in 1712. He spent the bulk of his career there and eventually became a naturalized citizen of Britain in 1727. He was greatly influenced both by the middle-German polyphonic choral tradition and by other composers of the Italian Baroque. Source
